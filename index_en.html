Laboratory Overview

Reproducing Life (Life Phenomena) on a Chip

At the Kaji Laboratory, we are developing new bioanalytical technologies by utilizing micro- and nano-spaces fabricated using semiconductor microfabrication techniques as a "field" for measuring and diagnosing biological substances and cells. Ultimately, we aim to expand these Lab-on-a-Chip (µTAS) analytical technologies to create Life-on-a-Chip, which can artificially reproduce life (life phenomena) on a chip.

We welcome individuals who:

  • Can work independently and enthusiastically on research
  • Have a pioneering spirit
  • Can accept diversity and have good coordination skills

Let's work together with the staff to ensure a meaningful student life while maintaining awareness that each person supports the laboratory.

Research

Development of Single Cell and Bacteria Analysis Methods

While information such as genetic sequence data from cells and bacteria can now be analyzed relatively easily and at low cost, these are mostly destructive analysis methods that cannot be applied to cell quality assurance in regenerative medicine. Therefore, in this research, we are developing a non-staining, non-destructive single-cell analysis method by developing microfluidic devices that can "touch and diagnose" cells, contributing to advanced medical analysis technology.

Development of Artificial Cells and Extracellular Vesicles Analysis Methods and Their Applications in Bioanalysis, Drug Discovery, and Medicine

Extracellular vesicles (EVs) released by cells, ranging from about 50 nm to several mm in diameter, contain nucleic acids and proteins from their originating cells and are abundantly present in body fluids. Particularly, exosomes are expected as new disease biomarkers that can be collected with low invasiveness. However, there is no established methodology for extraction and evaluation methods, and as they are highly heterogeneous substances, new analysis methods are required. Therefore, in this research, we are developing devices that can detect both the "particle" properties and "internal" information of EVs at the single EV level, and developing devices that can collect only useful EVs for medical applications.

Microfluidic Devices for Gut Microbiota Research

We maintain a healthy life by utilizing the functions of countless bacteria living in our intestines. However, the process of forming this bacterial symbiotic environment is not well understood. In this research, we aim to develop methods to observe when, where, and how bacteria settle in the body by creating an environment similar to the intestines using microfabrication technology.

Research on State-of-the-Art Analysis Technology Using Laser Light

We are researching original analysis technologies by combining new tools that have not been used in analytical chemistry with laser light sources developed independently. For example, the "Dispersion-Compensated High-Finesse Resonator" is a mechanism that we first proposed, which has the potential to dramatically improve the performance of analytical instruments. Through such approaches, we aim to develop ultimate analysis technologies using laser light. Furthermore, these methods can develop into devices with unprecedented functions, such as methods to modulate (give periodic changes to) laser light at the world's highest speed.
